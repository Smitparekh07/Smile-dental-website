Smoking Can Burn Your Teeth
Smoking has an impact on gum health because it reduces blood oxygen levels, which impedes the healing of infected gums. Compared to non-smokers, smoking increases tooth plaque and hastens the development of gum disease. Gum disease is still the main reason for adult tooth loss. It has been determined that compared to occasional smokers, who are more likely to lose one to two teeth every ten years, daily smokers are more likely to lose four to five teeth over the course of 17 years. According to this, persons who smoke regularly are at an increased chance of losing five or more teeth overall.
